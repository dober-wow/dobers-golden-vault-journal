Overview

Unbeknownst to anyone but a disgraced academic, the Varkenbluff Museum of Natural History is in grave danger. Archaeologists at a nearby dig site recently unearthed a curiosity: the Murkmire Stone.

This object is actually an eldritch creature's egg, and it's about to hatch. The characters must infiltrate the museum, steal the egg, and return it to Dr. Cassee Dannell to be neutralized before it releases an eldritch horror.

‚è∞ Time Pressure: The egg hatches at midnight. The gala begins at 6 PM, museum closes at 8 PM. Players have ~6 hours in-world.

Adventure Background

Dr. Cassee Dannell, a brilliant academic, discovered through her occult research that the recently unearthed Murkmire Stone is actually an eldritch creature's egg.

Key points:

  • The stone was found in the Murkmire by Varkenbluff University archaeologists
  • Dr. Dannell recognized it from her secret occult studies
  • The university dismissed her warnings as "pseudoscience"
  • She was caught trying to steal it and was fired
  • The egg hatches at midnight tonight

According to her research, these eggs lay dormant for generations until unearthed, then rapidly develop. The resulting creatures are ravenous for raw meat and grow exponentially. The only way to neutralize the egg is to encase it in specially prepared crystal.

No matter your business this morning, a grave halfling messenger finds you and hands you a sealed parchment.

"Meet me at the Sage's Quill today as soon as you can," the missive reads. "I beg your help in a delicate matter whose importance cannot be overstated. I shall await you in a purple hooded robe."

The Meeting

The Sage's Quill is a quiet, plush tavern adjacent to the museum. Dr. Dannell (NG human commoner, INT 18) provides:

  • Gala tickets for each character
  • Formal attire from her heirloom bag of holding
  • Her sketch of the museum (incomplete)
  • The bag of holding itself (to store gear)

Reward

For bringing the stone back: The bag of holding + 20 gp per character (30 gp with DC 13 Persuasion).

Security Measures

Audible alarm spells are placed on:

  • Hallways from V1 to V3
  • Doors from V1 to offices (V5, V6, V7)

  • Door from V12 to V13

Statues in V1 and V12 animate (use animated armor stat block) if anyone comes within 5 feet after hours.

Gemstone Wing Trap

Removing the stone from its pedestal triggers arcane lock on all doors (DC 20 to pick/force). Can be circumvented by swapping with a fake stone from V9 (DC 10 Sleight of Hand).

Conclusion

Mission Success

Dr. Dannell locks the stone in her crystal box, neutralizing it. She tries to regain her position at the university.

Rewards:

  • Bag of holding + 20-30 gp per character
  • If using Golden Vault: uncommon magic item of their choice

Mission Failure

If they don't deliver by midnight:

  • The egg hatches into an 8-foot-long nascent eldritch horror
  • Use ankheg stat block (poison bite instead of acid)
  • It terrorizes the museum, eating guards

Further Adventures

If Successful

  • Another Stone: Second egg found at another dig site, closer to hatching
  • Zoo Hatchling: Strange creature at Varkenbluff Zoo hatched from similar egg
  • Reinstate Dr. Dannell: Curator Alda Arkin is secretly a crime lord fencing stolen artifacts

If Unsuccessful

  • Stolen Stone: Alda steals it for her syndicate, transforms into a Monstrosity
  • Feeding Frenzy: Hatchling grows to pony-size in days, then breaks out. Uses behir stat block when fully grown.

The Murkmire Stone

Recently unearthed from the Murkmire outside Varkenbluff, the Murkmire Stone is actually the egg of an eldritch creature. The opaque, light-green stone weighs 10 pounds and has abstract furrows on its surface. It is a nonmagical object (it's an egg, not an artifact).

Activation Timeline

The egg's removal from the Murkmire rapidly accelerated the development of the creature inside.

  • 10:30 PM: Stony shell becomes translucent, revealing the horror inside. Psychic pulses begin.
  • Every 10 minutes: Emits a pulse of magical energy.
  • Midnight: The egg hatches.

Pulse Mechanics

Whenever a pulse occurs, each creature within 20 feet must make a DC 10 Wisdom saving throw. A creature holding the egg has disadvantage on the roll.

üì¶ Bag of Holding: If the egg is placed in a bag of holding or other portable extradimensional space, the effect radiates from the container instead.

When a creature fails the save, roll on the table below. When a creature rolls for a new effect, the previous one ends. All effects end when the egg hatches or is encased in Dr. Dannell's crystal box.

DM Tips to Avoid TPK

  1. Stealth is the answer. Remind players they're thieves, not soldiers.
  2. Guards capture, not kill. Dropped PCs are stabilized and restrained.
  3. Reinforcements arrive slowly. 1-2 rounds to flee before backup.
  4. The Allosaurus is a distraction. Smart players use it to draw guards.
  5. Hatched horror = chase scene. "Escape before it eats you."
