Session 4: Reach for the Stars

Date: February 7, 2026
Location: Varkenbluff → Forest Road → Delphi Mansion (threshold)
Adventure: Reach for the Stars — Bridge Session (Keys From The Golden Vault)

Previously...

The Stygian Gambit ended in blood and gold. The party robbed Quentin Togglepocket's vault of 9,000 gold pieces, stole the Golden Erinyes statuette, and escaped across the river — but not before Freya slit the throat of Tim, the tiefling ferrier who'd helped them flee.

Now they're in a stagecoach, heading back toward Varkenbluff with a fortune in stolen gold and an uncomfortable silence between them.

Act 1: The Stagecoach

The session opened with the narrator recap — the full weight of what happened last session delivered in a single breath.

Inside the stagecoach, the rift was palpable. Slim sat in the far corner, arms crossed, Beauregard hunched on his shoulder — cold fury, refusing to look at Freya. Freya sat opposite, staring at her own hands. Blood Feather watched both of them. Maltus stared out the window, pretending not to notice. Kiara curled up on the bench, tail wrapped around herself.

Freya showed some regret about Tim, but no one forced a resolution. The rift stayed open — simmering, not boiling.

Act 1: Meeting Verity — The Payout

Verity Kye was waiting at The Sage's Quill. Professional as always.

"So how did it go?"

The payout: 4,000 gold pieces in marked casino currency, returned to the party. Verity kept her 5,000 gold cut. The party asked about buying the Bag of Holding — Verity shut that down cold.

On operational security:

"I had to maintain some operational security — nothing personal, just been burned too much."

Then came the Quentin warning:

"Quentin won't forget this. You embarrassed him in front of the tournament crowd, emptied his vault, AND stole his prize piece. He's petty, he's connected, and he has a direct line to the Nine Hells. Stay out of Varkenbluff for a while."

Someone brought up Tim. Verity's response was ice.

"I didn't ask for that."

Act 2: Cassee Dannell — The Road Encounter

After leaving The Sage's Quill, the corrupted doctor appeared on the road. She stood unnaturally still — a puppet with cut strings.

Krokulmar spoke through her first:

"You left her. In the dark. With the dreaming thing. The egg sleeps. But it remembers. It whispers to us now. To HER now."

Then a moment of clarity — Cassee, the real Cassee, breaking through:

"Where am I? I was... I was in my study. The crystal box was glowing. And then... You. I asked for your help. You refused."

Krokulmar returned — one final message:

"There is a house. In the forest. He reads from a book of stars. You cannot stop what is already dreaming."

The party watched in shock. No one attempted to restrain her. She vanished.

The Long Evening — Drydock Cellar

Back in their underground hideout — the Drydock Cellar, inside the wooden ribs of an unfinished ship hull. Brass lanterns on hooks. A copper kettle steaming on a brazier. The calm after the storm.

Maltus recalled that casinos mark their currency — flagging the 4,000 gold pieces as traceable. Any spending over 50 gp risks detection.

Slim revealed a contact: Igor, who can launder gold through The Crucible — Greta Irontongue's river barge. He placed a filed coin at a tavern to summon Igor.

And in the quiet, Blood Feather heard a whisper:

"The egg sleeps. But it dreams."

The Morning After — Varkenbluff Errands

The party went topside for supplies and information — carefully.

  • Slim placed a filed coin at a tavern to summon Igor (the laundering contact)
  • Blood Feather purchased two potions using clean (non-marked) currency
  • The party noticed wanted posters — charcoal sketches of "the cowboy" and associates are circulating
  • No marked gold was spent. The party was cautious.

Act 3: The Music Box Briefing

Molly delivered a parcel at The Sage's Quill. Inside: an ornate golden music box. When opened, it delivered the voice of the Golden Vault — grim news.

"Operatives. We come to you with a burden, not a request."

"A sage named Vasil Talistrome — a friend of the Vault — has reported the theft of a book called The Celestial Codex..."

"We sent four people to retrieve it. Good people. Experienced. They entered Delphi Mansion and did not come out."

"Come back. That is not a request either."

Act 3: Meeting Vasil Talistrome

Vasil — an elderly tiefling scholar — met the party at The Sage's Quill. He was drowning in correspondence, half-eaten stew, and grief.

Markos Delphi

Vasil told them about his former student — Markos Delphi. Brilliant. Obsessive. A man who reached too far into the multiverse and contacted something from the Far Realm.

The Celestial Codex

The book itself: star maps and summoning rituals. Warnings misused as instructions. Markos stole it from Vasil's tower and retreated to his family estate — Delphi Mansion.

Elra Lionheart

Vasil hired a team of four — led by Elra Lionheart — to retrieve the Codex. Divination confirmed what he feared: they were dead. He carries the guilt.

Tactical Intel

Vasil provided what he knew:

  • "Destroy the anchors" — crystals deep in the basement
  • "The mansion may be warped" — eldritch surges when magic is used
  • "Reach Markos" — a Persuasion check may break the entity's hold

Reward: 100 gold per character for the Codex. An extra 150 if Markos is returned alive.

Departure

"Don't let that be the end of his story."

Act 4: The Forest Road — Elra's Ghost

The forest road to Delphi Mansion. The atmosphere shifted — dense ancient trees, heavy fog, the last light of dusk dying between gnarled oaks. Within one mile of the mansion, a spectral head appeared — floating at eye level, translucent blue-white.

Elra Lionheart. Dead, but not gone.

She delivered a tactical briefing from beyond the grave — calm, matter-of-fact, almost wryly amused.

Mansion Warnings

"The mansion itself, mostly. It's... wrong. Rooms change. The air fights you."

Creatures: "Puddles of eyes and mouths" — gibbering mouthers. Something with "hooks for hands."

The Camp

"We had a camp. On a bluff due south of the mansion..."

Her backpack was still there — with a map and notes left by cultists.

Spellcasting Warning

"If someone casts a spell inside, it triggers these effects."

A Soldier's Last Request

"I'd like to see my body again, if you find it."

Elra confirmed she would accompany the party into the mansion.

Session End: Elra's Camp

The party arrived at a rocky bluff overlooking the forested valley. Through a gap in the trees: Delphi Mansion — a grand three-story stone manor, dark against the twilight sky. No lights. The angles slightly off, the shadows too deep.

In Elra's backpack, they found the mansion map and three cultist notes:

  • "Markos has a room on the third floor where he performs rituals."
  • "A cavernous room underneath the basement contains four large crystals — purpose unknown."
  • "Mansion windows can be unlocked using the password 'Krokulmar.'"
